National Progams
As a Christian support organization, Prison Fellowship has international programs and classes aimed at assisting ex-convicts in adjusting to post-prison society and advocating for criminal justice reform.

Volunteers of America is a nationwide program that covers a wide variety of needs for ex-convicts along with many other economically and socially challenged demographics. Some of the groups they cover include disabled, mentally ill, and homeless people along with children of ex-convicts.
The U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development specializes in providing adequate housing and conditions for impoverished "US citizens and specific categories of non-citizens who have eligible immigration status" (Housing Choice Vouchers Fact Sheet). There are some limitations to housing for ex-felons based on the nature of the crime. For more information on housing for ex-convicts check here.
Illinois Programs
Jobs for Felons is a national organization that creates databases of jobs within specific cities. The organization has a branch for jobs specific to Chicago and a wide variety of options to choose from.
The Chicagoland Prison Outreach program provides support and outreach for convicts both during and after serving their sentence with both support and reintegration groups and letters to inmates during their sentences.
St. Leonard's Ministries provides temporary housing to ex-convicts while they gain footing and adjust to life after their prison sentence. It also provides psychological counseling through courses and planned treatment.
